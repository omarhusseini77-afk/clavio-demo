What it is

Clavio is the data standardisation and intelligence layer beneath private equity reporting. It connects to a portfolio company's accounting system, standardises the financial figures to one consistent model, and makes that clean data available for two things: investor-ready LP reporting with plain-language analysis, and a natural language AI assistant that lets both the GP and LPs ask direct questions about the numbers.

It removes the manual extraction and reformatting work that fund teams do every quarter to turn raw portfolio company numbers into investor statements - and replaces the static report with a live, queryable data layer.

Portfolio Company
Connects accounting system or uploads documents. Clavio extracts and standardises. Reviews and submits each quarter.
GP / Fund Manager
Full portfolio view, trends, anomaly flags, and an AI assistant to query any portfolio company's financials in plain language.
LP / Investor
Capital account, fund performance (IRR, MOIC, TVPI, DPI), and their own AI assistant scoped to investor-appropriate data.
The problem

Mid-market GPs still assemble investor reporting by hand. Each portfolio company reports differently, in different systems and formats. Associates re-key figures into Excel, reconcile them, and rebuild the same LP statements every quarter. The work is slow, error-prone, and scales badly past a handful of holdings.

Who it is for

European mid-market PE firms: funds of roughly EUR 50M to 500M, France and UK focus, EBITDA-driven SME buyouts. A segment the incumbent platforms - Allvue, Carta, iLevel, Juniper Square - serve poorly. They are priced and built for the institutional top end and handle French accounting systems weakly.

Why it is defensible
Integrations as the moat. Native connections to the accounting systems this segment actually uses - including Sage and Cegid - built directly rather than through middleware.
Bilingual by design. Full French and English operation across data, reporting, analysis, and AI responses - a real barrier for US-built incumbents.
Segment fit. Built specifically for the EBITDA-buyout mid-market, not a stripped-down version of an institutional tool.
Pricing model. Structured for fund sizes the incumbents overlook.

AI extraction is table stakes now - the whole category moved there in the past year. It is necessary, not a differentiator. The edges above are what hold up.

Where it stands
Confirmed pilot partner. A mid-market PE Managing Partner (London and Paris) has committed to a pilot, with a named portfolio company in his fund as the first live case.
Pilot scoped. First integration is QuickBooks, then standardisation, AI analysis, and one quarterly LP report. Data security is the partner's stated hard requirement and is the spine of the build plan.
Working demo live. Three role-based progressive web apps (portfolio company, GP, investor) deployed and accessible from any device, running on synthetic data with bilingual AI assistant active in all three views.
Build plan defined. Phased path from demo to live pilot with real authentication, data isolation (Row-Level Security), and live accounting integration - with clear delineation of AI-built vs. human-owned components.
Pilot objective
A tightly-scoped pilot build on one portfolio company that converts the GP partner from pilot to paying, and proves the model for the wider European mid-market PE segment.
